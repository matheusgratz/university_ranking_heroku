Our Purpose

This web site is design to help you to get a fast visualization of the quality of different universities around the world according to the ranking presented by The world university rankings.

The Times Higher Education World University Rankings (THE ) includes more than 1,500 universities across 93 countries, standing as the largest and most diverse university rankings ever to date. The table is based on 13 carefully calibrated performance indicators that measure an institution’s performance across teaching, research, knowledge transfer and international outlook. THE have created university rankings to assess university performance on the global stage and to provide a resource for readers to understand the different missions and successes of higher education institutions. Research rankings The THE world Rankings, founded in 2004, provide the definitive list of the world’s best universities, with an emphasis on the research mission. The ranking is related with an overall score for each university, which is comopsed by five indicators. Each indicator has a different weigh inside the overall score. The performance indicators are grouped into five areas: Teaching (the learning environment); Research (volume, income and reputation); Citations (research influence); International outlook (staff, students and research); and Industry income (knowledge transfer). Each indicator is expalined in the carousel below. Each university in the rankings has a detailed profile, with a breakdown of its overall scores across the rankings and supplementary data designed to help students. For example, there are data on the staff-to-student ratio at each university, the total income per student, the proportion of international students and the gender breakdown of students. If you wish to know more about the methodology to get the overall score and each indicator visit the oficial web site.



The different indicators

Teaching (30%)

In this indicator it is examined the perceived prestige of institutions in teaching and research. It has five sub-dimensions, which are:

  • Reputation survey: 15%
  • Staff-to-student ratio: 4.5%
  • Doctorate-to-bachelor’s ratio: 2.25%
  • Doctorates-awarded-to-academic-staff ratio: 6%
  • Institutional income: 2.25%

Research: 30%

This indicator looks at a university’s reputation for research excellence
among its peers, and it is composed by:

  • Reputation survey: 18%
  • Research income: 6%
  • Research productivity: 6%


Citations: 30%

The research influence indicator looks at universities’ role in spreading
new knowledge and ideas. It is examined research influence by capturing
the average number of times a university’s published work is cited by
scholars globally.


International outlook: 7.5%

This indicator measures the ability of a university to attract undergraduates,
postgraduates and faculty from all over the planet, and it is composed by:

  • Proportion of international students: 2.5%
  • Proportion of international staff: 2.5%
  • International collaboration: 2.5%%
Industry income: 2.5%

A university’s ability to help industry with innovations, inventions
and consultancy has become a core mission of the contemporary global academy.
This category seeks to capture such knowledge-transfer activity by looking at
how much research income an institution earns from industry (adjusted for PPP),
scaled against the number of academic staff it employs.
